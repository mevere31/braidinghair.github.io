Carcinogens Detected in Synthetic Hair

Carciogens are chemicals that may cause cancer. All of the synthetic hair brands tested contained multiple carcinogens. Three products contained benzene: Sensationnel Ruwa 3X Pre-Stretched Kids Braid 12", Sensationnel X-Pression 3X Pre-Stretched Braid 58", Magic Fingers 3X Pre-Stretched Braiding Hair, 52", which is a known carcinogen that causes acute myeloid leukemia. The list below lists all of the known, probable, and possible carcinogens that were detected. Visit Consumer Report to see the full test results.

  • Known Chemicals
    • Benzene
    • Litte Tabby
  • Probable Chemicals
    • Chloroform
    • Methylene Chloride
    • Styrene
    • 1,2-Dicholoroethane
    • Tetrachloride
  • Possible Chemicals
    • Napthalane
    • Ethylbenzene
    • Isopropylbenzene

Lead in Synthetic Braiding Hair

Heavy metals are found in a variety of food and products. Apparently, synthetic braiding hair is no different. Lead was detected in 9 of the 10 products tested. Over time, prolonged exposure to lead can damage the brain and nervous system and has been linked to immune system suppression, reproductive issues, kidney damage, and hypertension.

"MADL" meands "Maximum Allowable Dose Level."

Volatile Organic Chemicals in Synthetic Braiding Hair

Volatile Organic Chemicals are human-made chemicals used to make various products and emitted as gases. According to The American Lung Association, volatile organic chemicals can irritate the eyse, nose, and throat, can cause nausea, and damage the central nervous system. Consumer Report's tests showed that VOCS were emitted when the braiding hair was heated to boiling in the lab.
